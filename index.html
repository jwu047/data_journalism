General Observations

Obesity, smoking, and lacking healthcare percentage all positively correlate with poverty percentage. From our visualization, we see that smoking has a slightly more positively correlated with poverty than the other two. While it seems older populations generally tend to have a higher population that smokes, they also seem to not lack healthcare, or have a negative correlation.

When looking at percentage of the population that smokes and percentage in poverty, we see a positive correlation. Utah does seem to be an extreme value in this case with only 11.7% of the population in poverty and only having 9.7% of the population smoke. Since household income and poverty percentage has a negative correlation. Percentage of the population that smokes does tend to decrease as the household median income increases.

As the rate of poverty increases, the population tend to have a higher obesity rate, have a population that more generally smokes, and lack healthcare. It seems that Utah has a younger population that while, has an average obesity rate, has a lower population that smokes. Meanwhile Texas, also having a younger population has a higher than average obesity rate, but more noticeably has a population that lacks healthcare. While only having a median population age of around 34, 24.9% of the population does not have healthcare. Thankfully this data is in 2014 and around 2019, Texas has seen major improvements regarding its population's healthcare.
